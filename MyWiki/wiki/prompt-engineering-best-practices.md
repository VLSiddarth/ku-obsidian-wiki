## What this is
[[Prompt engineering]] best practices are a collection of strategies and guidelines for designing effective prompts that optimize the performance and output quality of [[Large Language Models]] (LLMs). The goal is to elicit precise, relevant, and desired responses by clearly communicating intent and constraints to the model, minimizing ambiguity and errors.

## Key ideas
Effective [[Prompt engineering]] prioritizes clarity, specificity, and structured input. Core practices include providing detailed context, explicit instructions, and specific constraints on the output (e.g., length, format like JSON or bullet points, tone). Assigning a "persona" or "role" to the LLM can guide its response style. Incorporating [[few-shot examples]] within the prompt demonstrates the expected output pattern, significantly enhancing consistency and accuracy. For complex tasks, employing [[Chain-of-Thought Prompting - Explained]] encourages the model to generate intermediate reasoning steps, leading to more robust and accurate final answers. Iterative refinement, where prompts are continually adjusted based on observed model responses, is essential for optimizing performance.

## Why it matters
Implementing [[Prompt Engineering Best Practices ]]significantly enhances the utility of LLMs by reducing irrelevant or incorrect outputs and minimizing the need for manual post-processing. It improves the efficiency of model interactions, conserves computational resources, and is crucial for developing reliable [[AI automation]] and sophisticated [[agentic workflows]].
