## What this is
Text expansion in [[prompt engineering]] is a technique used to instruct a [[large language model]] (LLM) to generate more detailed, elaborate, or lengthier output from a concise initial prompt. It aims to prevent overly brief or generic responses, guiding the LLM to fully leverage its knowledge base for comprehensive content generation.

## How it works
This practice involves crafting prompts that explicitly or implicitly guide the LLM toward greater output length and depth. Common strategies include specifying desired word counts, paragraph numbers, or structural elements (e.g., "expand this into a 500-word article," "write a three-paragraph explanation"). Prompts can also request the LLM to elaborate on specific points, provide examples, explore different perspectives, or adopt a persona to enrich the narrative. [[Few-shot prompting]], where examples of desired expanded text are provided, can also guide the model's output style and length.

## Why it matters
Text expansion is crucial for applications requiring substantive and rich content, such as generating articles, detailed reports, marketing copy, or creative narratives. It empowers users to produce extensive outputs from minimal input, reducing the need for multiple iterative prompts to add detail. This technique helps overcome the LLM's tendency to provide terse answers, ensuring that the generated text meets specific informational or creative requirements.
